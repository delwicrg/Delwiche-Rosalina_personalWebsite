About Me

I found my calling junior year of high school. I enrolled in computer-aided design as an opportunity to hone new skills. After successfully completing the course, I longed for more. It opened me to the field of technology and self-discovery. In a quest for self-discovery, I explored computer science through seeking outside resources and classes. From there, I knew computer science is for my field.

More than four years later, I am proud to say I am a senior Computer Science major with a minor in Mathematics at Clarkson University. Throughout my time at Clarkson, I have learned a wide array of computer languages including C, C++, Java, R, HTML, and CSS. Outside of the classroom, I am learning ReactJS and TypeScript. My motto in life is that learning never ends. There are many technologies I have yet to explore and many new emerging. The technology field excites me.

Aside from computer science, my passions include baking, cooking, drawing, and designing, as well as spending time with friends, family, and animals. Although seemingly unrelated, I combine my passions and experiences to give me a new perspective. Prior experiences foster new experiences.

As I end my senior year, I look forward to joining the workforce as a driven individual eager to change the world.

Interactive Scene

GitHub, and Scene Embedded

Instructions: There are three objects in which you can interact with: the right cup, the left cup, and the teapot. For all three, you may scale the object (aka increase size), translate up, down, left, right and also rotate. For the rotations, the teapot rotates in the z direction to "pour" the tea while the cups rotate in the x direction as one would expect. Test it out by pressing the keys a, d, w, s, q, and e for the left cup!


This is a relastic scene of a dining room. Users have the ability to manipulate objects through translation, rotation, and scaling. It is accmomplished through setting tags on keypress.
