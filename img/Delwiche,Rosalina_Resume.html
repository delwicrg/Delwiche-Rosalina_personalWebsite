About Me

I found my calling junior year of high school. I enrolled in computer-aided design. While I could have chosen another elective, I considered this an opportunity to hone new skills. After successfully completing the course, I was longing for more. It opened me to the field of technology and self-discovery. In a quest for self-discovery in highschool, I took many college-level computer science courses. From there, I knew that computer science is my field.

More than four years later, I am proud to say I am a senior Computer Science major with a minor in Mathematics at Clarkson University. Throughout my time at Clarkson, I have learned a wide array of computer languages including C, C++, Java, R, HTML, and CSS. Outside of the classroom, I seek to learn more. I am currently learning ReactJS and TypeScript. My motto in life is that learning never ends. There are many technologies I have yet to explore and many new emerging. The technology field excites me.

Aside from computer science, my passions include baking, cooking, drawing, and designing, as well as spending time with friends, family, and animals. Although seemingly unrelated, I combine my passions and experiences to give me a new perspective. Sometimes coding can be like baking in a procedurual and creative stand point. Prior experiences foster new experiences.

As I end my senior year, I look forward to joining the workforce as a driven individual eager to change the world.
